# KuwaPet Visual Design System

## Creative Direction

KuwaPet should look like a premium pantry-meets-pet-wellness brand.

The visual language should feel:

- warm
- tactile
- orchard-inspired
- modern
- trustworthy
- minimal without feeling empty

It should not look like:

- a generic Shopify pet template
- a biotech supplement company
- a cartoon pet toy store
- an all-beige luxury brand with no personality

## Visual Mood Words

- mulberry jam
- oat cream
- orchard leaf
- ceramic bowl
- soft natural light
- elevated kitchen shelf
- quiet premium
- ingredient-first

## Art Direction Summary

The brand should feel like it belongs somewhere between a premium food brand and a thoughtful pet care company.

The site should use:

- clean editorial compositions
- close-up ingredient imagery
- real dogs in calm, lived-in environments
- tactile surfaces such as linen, ceramic, wood, matte paper, and brushed metal
- subtle curvature and organic shapes

## Color Palette

Use a distinct mulberry-led palette that avoids the default pet-brand blues.

### Primary Colors

- `Mulberry Jam` `#6A2143`
- `Mulberry Skin` `#8E365D`
- `Oat Cream` `#F5EEDF`
- `Bark` `#4B3A32`
- `Leaf` `#556B4F`

### Secondary Colors

- `Dusty Rose` `#C68FA2`
- `Warm Sand` `#D9C5A1`
- `Sage Mist` `#A9B8A0`
- `Clay` `#AA735D`

### Utility Colors

- `Ink` `#201A17`
- `Paper` `#FFFBF6`
- `Line` `#D8CCBD`
- `Success` `#4F6F52`
- `Warning` `#9A6A2F`

## Color Usage Rules

- Use `Oat Cream` or `Paper` as the main canvas.
- Use `Mulberry Jam` as the signature accent, not the full-page flood color.
- Use `Leaf` to support natural cues and secondary accents.
- Use `Bark` and `Ink` for text depth and premium contrast.
- Avoid overusing bright gradients or candy colors.

## Typography

Avoid default system-looking typography.
The brand should feel editorial and intentional.

### Recommended Pairing

- Headlines: `Fraunces`
- Body: `Manrope`

### Alternate Pairing

- Headlines: `Cormorant Garamond`
- Body: `Sora`

### Typography Rules

- Headlines should feel expressive and premium, with high visual contrast.
- Body copy should stay clean, modern, and highly readable.
- Use short line lengths for premium feel.
- Let headlines breathe.
- Avoid tiny text, especially on mobile.

## Type Scale Direction

- Hero headline: large, confident, likely 48px to 72px desktop
- Section headings: 32px to 48px desktop
- Subhead / body lead: 18px to 22px
- Standard body: 16px to 18px
- Microcopy: 13px to 14px minimum

## Layout Principles

- Use generous spacing.
- Favor asymmetry where it helps the brand feel designed rather than templated.
- Break up long vertical stacks with alternating media and text rhythms.
- Use rounded rectangles and soft organic containers instead of rigid sharp boxes everywhere.

## Grid Guidance

- Desktop: 12-column grid
- Tablet: 8-column grid
- Mobile: 4-column grid

Use:

- wide margins
- strong vertical spacing rhythm
- meaningful negative space around key moments

## Component Style

### Buttons

Primary buttons:

- filled mulberry background
- light text
- medium-large radius
- strong hover state with subtle lift

Secondary buttons:

- outlined or soft-fill neutral
- clearly secondary but still premium

Buttons should feel substantial, not tiny.

### Cards

Cards should feel soft, editorial, and tactile.

Use:

- rounded corners
- warm off-white backgrounds
- thin warm borders
- subtle shadows only where needed

### Pills / Badges

Use badges sparingly for:

- `Made for dogs`
- `Simple ingredient story`
- `Daily topper`

Badges should not resemble aggressive ecommerce discount labels unless a true promotion exists.

### FAQ Accordions

Keep them elegant and spacious.
Avoid overly mechanical accordion styling.

### Reviews

Review cards should feel calm and premium.
Use quotation marks sparingly and keep layouts airy.

## Photography Direction

### Product Photography

Prioritize:

- clean packshots
- powder texture close-ups
- scoop or sprinkle moments
- product next to dog bowl and meal context

### Dog Photography

Use dogs that feel:

- loved
- relaxed
- healthy-looking
- real rather than overly commercial

Prefer:

- natural home settings
- warm morning or afternoon light
- close crop moments with bowls, hands, and routines

Avoid:

- silly costume shots
- hyperactive stock-photo poses
- veterinary exam room imagery

### Ingredient Photography

Mulberry should be visually celebrated.

Use:

- fruit references
- powder texture macro shots
- ingredient on ceramic or matte stone surfaces
- subtle leaf or orchard cues

## Illustration Direction

Illustration can help if photography is limited.

Recommended style:

- hand-drawn botanical accents
- minimal line illustrations
- soft editorial ingredient diagrams

Avoid childish pet doodles.

## Motion Design

Motion should be intentional and quiet.

Recommended moments:

- fade / rise hero entrance
- staggered card reveal on scroll
- subtle image parallax or depth
- gentle hover movement on CTA buttons

Avoid:

- overanimated backgrounds
- spinning product mockups
- excessive scroll hijacking

## Background Treatments

Avoid a flat blank page.

Better options:

- soft radial wash behind hero content
- subtle grain texture
- faint botanical line pattern
- gentle tonal transitions from cream to blush to warm white

## Iconography

Use simple line icons or refined filled icons.

Potential icon set themes:

- scoop
- bowl
- leaf
- package
- question circle
- shipping box

Avoid playful cartoon paw icons unless extremely restrained.

## Homepage Visual Notes

### Hero

The hero should be the most emotionally powerful part of the site.

Recommended composition:

- bold left-aligned type
- product packshot plus bowl / dog image cluster
- soft mulberry-toned atmospheric background
- premium CTA cluster

### Why Mulberry Section

This is a chance for visual storytelling:

- ingredient close-up
- botanical framing
- textured background panel

### How To Use Section

Consider a three-step horizontal sequence on desktop and stacked cards on mobile.

## Product Page Visual Notes

- Keep purchase controls clean and elevated.
- Use sticky product summary on desktop if layout supports it.
- Let media feel tactile and zoomable.
- Keep informational tabs or accordions visually consistent with the rest of the brand.

## Accessibility Notes For Design

- Maintain strong contrast for body text.
- Do not rely on color alone for important states.
- Ensure buttons remain clearly visible against soft backgrounds.
- Use readable type sizes on mobile.
- Motion should respect reduced-motion preferences.

## Suggested Design Tokens

### Radius

- `radius-sm`: `10px`
- `radius-md`: `18px`
- `radius-lg`: `28px`
- `radius-xl`: `40px`

### Shadow

- `shadow-soft`: low blur, low opacity, warm tone
- `shadow-card`: subtle and short
- `shadow-pop`: reserved for CTA hover states only

### Spacing Rhythm

- small: `8px`
- base: `16px`
- medium: `24px`
- large: `40px`
- section: `72px` to `120px`

## Design Test

If a screenshot of the site is shown without context, it should still feel like:

- a premium direct-to-consumer brand
- a dog product, but not a toy brand
- rooted in mulberry and natural warmth
- more emotionally designed than a generic supplement page
