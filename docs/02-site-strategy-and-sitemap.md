# KuwaPet Site Strategy And Sitemap

## Strategic Goal

Build a focused single-product ecommerce site that:

- earns trust quickly
- teaches the user what the product is
- makes the ingredient story memorable
- creates a premium emotional experience
- drives purchase without overcomplicating the journey

## Primary Conversion Goal

Purchase the hero product.

## Secondary Conversion Goals

- join the email list
- read the FAQ
- understand how to use the product
- explore the ingredient story
- revisit later with high brand recall

## Key User Questions The Site Must Answer Fast

Within the first screen or two, visitors should know:

- What is KuwaPet?
- What is the product?
- Is it for dogs?
- How is it used?
- Why mulberry?
- Why should I trust this brand?
- What do I do next?

## Recommended Information Architecture

6. How to use

Purpose:

Recommended sections:

## Homepage Strategy

The homepage should not feel like a generic pet store.
It should feel like a premium single-product launch.

### Homepage Narrative Arc

1. Clarify the product immediately.
2. Create curiosity around mulberry.
3. Show daily ritual and ease of use.
4. Reinforce trust.
5. Invite purchase.

### Recommended Homepage Wireframe

#### Hero

Include:

- clear statement of what the product is
- one supporting line about intentional daily use
- primary CTA: `Shop Now`
- secondary CTA: `Learn More`
- strong product image plus dog / bowl context

#### Trust Strip

Keep this restrained.

Potential items:

- Made for dogs
- Simple ingredient story
- Easy daily topper
- Fast shipping placeholder

Do not include claims that need substantiation unless verified.

#### What It Is

A short explainer section with 3 cards:

- what it is
- how it fits into routine
- why it feels different from typical supplement clutter

#### Why Mulberry

This section should be educational but visually rich.

Use:

- ingredient photography or illustration
- a short narrative
- links to deeper ingredient page

#### How To Use

Show the ritual:

1. Scoop or sprinkle
2. Add to the meal
3. Serve

If exact serving details are not known, say `Feeding directions placeholder to be confirmed before launch`.

#### Brand Philosophy

This is where KuwaPet can say:

- less noise
- more intention
- simple ingredients
- a more thoughtful bowl ritual

#### Reviews

Only use real reviews.
If reviews are not yet available, use:

- press quotes if real
- brand values section
- photography-driven trust content

Do not invent testimonials.

#### FAQ Preview

Show 4 to 6 top questions with a link to the full FAQ page.

#### Final CTA

End with a strong purchase invitation and a visual reminder of the product.

## Product Page Strategy

The product page should convert buyers who already have interest.

### Must-Have Elements Above The Fold

- product name
- plain-language product descriptor
- price
- buy button
- quantity selector
- simple bullet proof points
- shipping or delivery reassurance if available

### Product Bullet Proof Point Ideas

Use only if true:

- Made for dogs
- Simple ingredient story
- Easy to add to daily meals
- Premium pantry-style format

### Product Page Section Order

1. Media gallery
2. Purchase box
3. Ingredient / product details
4. Usage instructions
5. Trust / policies
6. FAQ
7. Related content or About block

## Conversion Principles

- Keep the primary CTA visible often.
- Reduce copy clutter near the buy button.
- Put deeper education below the fold.
- Use repeated but tasteful CTAs.
- Make mobile add-to-cart friction very low.

## Mobile Experience Priorities

- sticky mobile buy bar after the hero product section
- fast-loading hero image
- tappable accordion FAQs
- comfortable font sizes
- no wall-of-text blocks
- cart and checkout buttons easy to reach with one thumb

## Future Expansion Path

The information architecture should allow future additions without redesigning from scratch:

- more topper products
- bundles
- subscriptions
- ingredient education library
- blog / journal
- retailer locator
- wholesale or partnership pages

If the site stays single-product at launch, `/shop` can route users directly to the hero product page.

## Success Criteria

The final site structure is successful if a first-time visitor can answer these questions within 10 seconds:

- This is a dog product.
- It is a mulberry-based topper.
- I understand roughly how to use it.
- The brand feels premium and trustworthy.
- I know where to click to buy.
